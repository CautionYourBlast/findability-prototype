The British Embassy in Madrid maintains and develops relations between the UK and Spain.

Getting help in Spain

If you need urgent medical assistance or are a victim of crime, call the local emergency services on 112. Contact your insurance company if you have it.

In most non-emergency situations, you will be able to help yourself without needing to contact the British Embassy. Check our guidance on:

  • lost or stolen passports or travel documents
  • getting an emergency travel document if your passport has been lost or stolen
  • being arrested in Spain
  • getting help if you’re a victim of crime
  • dealing with a death in Spain
  • travel advice for Spain
  • finding English-speaking lawyers, funeral directors and translators and interpreters in Spain

See all the help and services guidance for travelling and living in Spain. If you need advice which is not covered by this guidance you can contact us online.

If you need emergency assistance from the British Consulate

We can help British nationals in emergencies. We will prioritise helping those who are vulnerable and need our help the most. The help we can provide depends on the situation and the person’s circumstances. We cannot give legal advice or interfere in criminal or civil court proceedings in another country.

If you’re in:

  • Spain and need urgent help (for example, you’ve been attacked or arrested) call +34 91 714 6300
  • the UK and need assistance for someone in Spain call 020 7008 5000

Official documents and services we provide

In certain circumstances, we can provide notarial and documentary services for British nationals in Spain. Consular staff can:

  • administer an oath, affirmation or affidavit
  • make a certified copy of a British passport or UK degree certificate
  • unite documents
  • prepare a document in English or the local language

You will need to pay a fee for these services.

  • Las Palmas

  • Santa Cruz de Tenerife
