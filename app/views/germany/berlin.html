The British Embassy in Berlin maintains and develops relations between the UK and Germany.

We work closely with the German government on a wide range of issues, including climate change, science and innovation, foreign policy and defence and police co-operation.

Find out more on our UK and Germany news page.

Getting help in Germany

If you need urgent medical assistance or are a victim of crime, call the local emergency services on 112. Contact your insurance company if you have it.

In most situations, you will be able to help yourself without needing to contact the British Embassy. Check our guidance on:

  • finding English-speaking lawyers, funeral directors and translators and interpreters in Germany
  • dealing with a death in Germany
  • being arrested in Germany
  • getting help if you’re a victim of crime
  • travel advice for Germany

See all the help and services guidance for travelling and living in Germany. If you need advice which is not covered by this guidance you can contact us online.

If you need emergency assistance from the British Embassy

We can help British nationals in emergencies. We will prioritise helping those who are vulnerable and need our help the most. The help we can provide depends on the situation and the person’s circumstances. We cannot give legal advice or interfere in criminal or civil court proceedings in another country.

If you’re in:

  • Germany call 030 204 570
  • the UK call 020 7008 5000

If you can’t use your passport

You can apply for an emergency travel document if you need to travel urgently and can’t use your passport (for example, because it’s lost or damaged).

If you don’t need to travel urgently, you should still report a lost or stolen passport.

Getting a visa to the UK

The British Embassy does not have a visa section and cannot advise on visa matters.

A Schengen visa or residence permit does not entitle you to travel to the UK without a visa. You can check whether you need a visa and apply for one.

People who have fled the war in Ukraine can find more about how to apply for a Ukraine Family Scheme visa. You can also read about UK visa support for Ukrainian nationals.

If you have any questions about the application process or your visa status, contact UKVI.

Official documents and services we provide

In certain circumstances, we can provide notarial and documentary services for British nationals in Germany. Consular staff can:

  • administer an oath, affirmation or affidavit
  • witness a signature
  • make a certified copy of a British passport, Home Office registration/naturalisation certificate, UK educational certificate, British driving licence or British divorce decree
  • convert a civil partnership to marriage
  • legalise a UK document

You will need to pay a fee for these services.
